Peer Instruction: Active Learning ("flipped classroom") in CS

I have adopted Peer Instruction (PI), an active learning pedagogy.

In 2012, I worked with Leo Porter, Beth Simon, and others on Peer Instruction (PI), an active learning pedagogy initially developed by Eric Mazur at Harvard to teach introductory Physics. The PI community has shown that PI is effective in CS Education, including higher exam scores, lower WDF rates, and higher retention in the major.

Knoxcraft: Code that builds 3D stuff in Minecraft for Intro CS!

Students write code in Java, Python, or Blockly that builds 3D structures. Then they run the code in Minecraft to visualize their structures.

Learn more at knoxcraft.org!

Try Knoxcraft directly in your web browser at knoxcraft.github.io!

Cloudcoder: Short programming exercises in your browser

Cloudcoder is a collaboration with David Hovemeyer at York College. It is an open source system that lets students solve short programming exercises in the web browser, with a 'submit' button that gives immediate feedback.

Cloudcoder also has an open repository of short programming exercises. Math faculty have been assigning problems from question banks for decades; why can't we do the same?

Learn more at cloudcoder.org!

Startup Term: Entrepreneurship in the Liberal Arts

I worked with John Dooley and John Spittel to design Startup Term, a 10-week immersive experience. Teams of students submit a business and marketing plan and "pitch" their startup idea. If accepted, instead of attending classes for a term, they show up to an office suite in downtown Galesburg and (with some advice from us) handle everything a small business would need to deal with: partnership agreements, banking, intellectual property, terms of service (if they are running a web service), market research, financial projections; you name it, they have to do it.

At the end of the term, the have to pitch their business to a panel of judges, including local business leaders, Knox alumni entrepreneurs, and members of Knox's Board of Trustees.
